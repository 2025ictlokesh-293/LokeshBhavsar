Ganesha is the beloved elephant-headed Hindu god of beginnings, wisdom, and good fortune, known as the "Remover of Obstacles" (Vighneshvara), worshipped before new ventures for success, wisdom, and as the patron of arts, sciences, and intellect. Son of Shiva and Parvati, he is easily identified by his elephant head, pot belly, and four arms, often holding sweets (laddus), an axe, and a noose, with a mouse as his vehicle, symbolizing his ability to overcome any obstacle. He is a pan-Hindu deity, significant in Jainism and Buddhism, and revered globally as the Lord of Beginnings and Success.
